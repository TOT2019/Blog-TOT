Understanding **how much does it cost to design an app** is one of the first things any business or startup needs to figure out before kicking off a digital product. The answer isn't a single number — it depends on what you're building, where your designer is based, and how polished you want the final product to be. This guide breaks it all down clearly so you can plan your budget with confidence.


## **What App Design Actually Covers**

Before looking at price tags, it helps to know what you're paying for. App design is more than choosing colors and fonts. It spans several interconnected phases:

- **UX research and user flows:** Understanding your users' needs, pain points, and behaviors. Designers map out how users navigate the app from start to finish.

- **Wireframes and prototypes:** Low-fidelity sketches of screen layouts, followed by interactive prototypes that simulate app behavior before any visuals are built.

- **UI design:** The visual layer covering color palettes, typography, buttons, icons, illustrations, and overall brand expression.

- **Micro-animations and interactive prototypes:** Transitions, hover effects, and gesture interactions that make the app feel alive and responsive.

- **Design handoff and post-launch updates:** Preparing asset packages and documentation for developers, plus ongoing UI improvements after launch.


## **How Much Does It Cost to Design an App by Complexity?**

Cost scales directly with how complex your app is. Here's a practical breakdown across three tiers.


### **Simple App Design**

Ideal for utility apps, landing-style apps, or informational tools with just a few screens.

- Scope: 5–10 screens, basic navigation, minimal user flows

- Estimated cost: $3,000 – $10,000

- Timeline: 2–4 weeks


### **Medium-Complexity App Design**

Covers most startup MVPs and business apps with interactive components.

- Scope: 10–20 screens, onboarding flows, dashboards, forms, light animations

- Estimated cost: $10,000 – $30,000

- Timeline: 4–8 weeks


### **Complex App Design**

Enterprise-level platforms, marketplaces, fintech, or healthcare apps with advanced UX requirements.

- Scope: 20+ screens, multi-platform design (iOS + Android), advanced workflows, custom branding, scalability planning

- Estimated cost: $30,000 – $80,000+

- Timeline: 8–16+ weeks


## **Key Factors That Drive App Design Costs**

When people ask how much does it cost to design an app, they often don't realize how many variables are at play. Here's what actually moves the price.

**1. Number of screens and complexity** More screens means more wireframes, more UI states, and more testing rounds. A 5-screen app and a 30-screen app are in completely different budget categories.

**2. Platform choice** Designing for iOS alone is one scope of work. Adding Android means following a separate design system. Adding a web version expands the effort again. Multi-platform development can double or triple design hours.

**3. Industry requirements** Healthcare, finance, and logistics apps often require specialized data views, compliance-aware UI patterns, and complex user flows. These raise the cost significantly compared to a simple e-commerce or content app.

**4. Designer location and experience** Hourly rates vary widely by region. Designers in North America and Western Europe typically charge $70–$150/hour. Eastern Europe ranges from $40–$75/hour. Southeast Asia sits between $20–$45/hour. Senior designers and established agencies also command higher rates, but they typically deliver fewer revisions and stronger outcomes.

**5. Level of UI/UX customization** Custom illustrations, original icon sets, branded motion design, and unique design systems cost significantly more than adapting an existing UI kit or template.

**6. Iteration rounds and user testing** Each round of usability testing and design revision adds time and cost. Complex apps may go through 3–5 testing cycles before the design is finalized.

**7. Ongoing maintenance** Post-launch design doesn't stop. UI refreshes, new feature screens, and design consistency work should be factored into any long-term budget. Many agencies offer monthly design support retainers.


## **Cost Breakdown by Design Phase**

Not all design dollars are spent equally. Here's how budgets typically distribute across each stage.

- **Discovery and research (10–20%):** User personas, competitor analysis, UX goals. A strong foundation here reduces expensive rework later.

- **Wireframing and UX mapping (15–25%):** Screen layouts, user journeys, and information architecture. This phase defines how the app flows before any visuals are applied.

- **UI design and branding (30–40%):** Visual design, design system, and brand integration. This is the largest spend area and where the product's look and feel comes to life.

- **Prototyping and user testing (15–20%):** Interactive prototypes and usability testing sessions. Early testing saves money by catching issues before development begins.

- **Developer handoff (5–10%):** Asset exports, style guides, and interaction specs to ensure developers build exactly what was designed.


## **How to Estimate Your App Design Budget**

If you're building a budget before reaching out to agencies, here's a practical step-by-step approach.

1. **Define your scope.** Are you building an MVP to validate an idea, or a full-featured product? An MVP with 8–10 core screens keeps costs manageable. A full product with 30+ screens is a different project entirely.

2. **List all required screens.** Write out every screen you need: onboarding, login, home, settings, key feature screens, error states, and empty states. This list is the foundation of any accurate estimate.

3. **Estimate hours per stage.** A simple app typically requires 80–120 design hours total. A complex app may need 300–500 hours. Multiply your estimated hours by the hourly rate of your designer or agency to get a rough project cost.

4. **Account for revisions.** Build in at least 15–20% extra budget for revisions and unexpected scope changes. Most projects require more iteration than initially planned.

5. **Prepare before you quote.** Agencies give more accurate estimates when you come with a clear brief, reference designs, existing brand assets, and a defined list of screens and features.


## **Smart Ways to Reduce App Design Costs**

Cutting costs doesn't have to mean cutting corners. These strategies help reduce spend while maintaining quality.

- **Start with an MVP.** Design only what's needed to validate your concept. You can expand the design system once the core product proves its value.

- **Use established design systems.** Material Design and Apple's Human Interface Guidelines offer ready-made components. Adapting these to your brand is faster and cheaper than building from scratch.

- **Launch on one platform first.** Start with iOS or Android, not both. Validate with real users, then expand.

- **Prepare a detailed brief upfront.** Clear requirements mean fewer revision rounds. Every round of unexpected revision adds cost.

- **Prototype early with low-fidelity tools.** Using Figma or Adobe XD to validate flows early catches UX problems before they reach the expensive UI design stage.

- **Outsource strategically.** Offshore teams in Southeast Asia or Eastern Europe can deliver high-quality work at 40–60% lower rates than Western agencies, especially for early-stage products.

## **Frequently Asked Questions**

**How much does a single app screen cost to design?** Typically $50–$400 per screen, depending on complexity, the designer's experience level, and how custom the interactions are.

**How much does it cost to launch an app on the App Store?** Apple charges $99/year for a developer account. Development and design costs are separate and typically range from $20,000 to $300,000+ depending on scope.

**What does a full app development cost breakdown include?** A complete budget covers UX/UI design, frontend and backend development, QA testing, project management, infrastructure such as APIs and hosting, app store submission, and post-launch maintenance.


## **Final Thoughts**

So, how much does it cost to design an app? For most businesses, expect to budget anywhere from $3,000 for a simple MVP to $80,000+ for a polished, multi-platform product. The exact number depends on your app's complexity, target platforms, and the experience level of the team you hire.

The best investment you can make before getting quotes is defining your scope clearly — know your screens, your users, and your core features. That clarity alone can save thousands in revision costs and ensure you get an accurate estimate from day one.
